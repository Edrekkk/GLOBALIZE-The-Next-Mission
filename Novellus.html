DYCI Novellus

The official student organization for avid readers who are passionate about promoting literacy and a love for the English language. The organization supports fellow DYClans who struggle with reading by offering peer-led reading guidance and encouragement, fostering a more inclusive and empowered learning environment.

Accomplishments

Novellus wraps dual shoots

DYCI Novellus have successfully completed the filming of Episode 3 and Episode 4. Episode 3, filmed on September 12, 2025, featured Mikaela Delos Reyes (12 – Wojtyla) discussing Dekada ‘70 by Lualhati Bautista. Episode 4, filmed on September 15, 2025, highlighted Arianne Jewel Mandap (12 – Rawls) with a script by Jasmine Eizelle Dipad (11 – Bregoglio), focusing on Days at the Morisaki Bookshop.

October shines for Novellus

The month of October has been a remarkable one for DYCI Novellus and the entire Dr. Yanga's Colleges, Inc. This month, the school achieved another milestone as Dr. Yanga's Colleges, Inc. received the Scholastic Readers Cup Achievement Award at the 2024-2025 Keaders Cup, held on October 15, 2025, at Crowne Plaza Manila Galleria.

BOOK QUEST: 1

Lit Link project continues to flourish, now reaching a total of 505 posts, each showcasing the creativity, insight, and literary enthusiasm of DYClan readers.

Book Quest 1 Milestone

In addition to the filming, we also recorded 256 additional Book Quest 1 postings from members, where they shared their favorite books, the titles they have read, and their recommendations for fellow readers.

EPISODES

Alongside this achievement, DYCI Novellus continued to make significant progress in its ongoing projects. The team successfully filmed Episodes 5 and 6 of Book Trivia, while Episodes 3, 4, and 5 were officially posted this month, engaging students with fun and educational content that celebrates literature. The organization also completed the writing and planning for Episodes 7 and 8, marking another step forward in its dedication to producing creative and meaningful materials that inspire a love for reading.

BOOK CORNER BOOTH

In addition, DYCI Novellus successfully conducted the Book Corner Booth as part of the "Flight 143" event. Members of the organization lent their personal books to create a cozy mini reading space where students could browse and enjoy different stories and genres. The booth also featured the sale of pins and stickers inspired by favorite titles, genres, authors, and books, giving students a creative way to express their passion for literature.
